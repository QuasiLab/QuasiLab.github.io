High-Energy Physics Computation

The High Energy Physics Computation research group is part of the LIPI Team in the ALICE (A Large Ion Collider Experiment) Collaboration. Members of the group participated in the early commissioning of the ALICE ITS (Inner Tracking System) Upgrade. On the physics side, members of the group worked on the physics analysis of heavy-flavour meson production, specifically D-meson production. We also have branched into phenomenology, studying application of multivariate analysis into searches of new particles, in collaboration with theorists.

We recently initiated a new activity in muon tomography by building key elements for muon detection using organic plastic scintillators. Muon tomography is a very promising technique to image the inner part of a large structure, with enormous potential applications e.g. in volcanology, archaeology, civil engineering, nuclear safety, and geophysical prospecting.
